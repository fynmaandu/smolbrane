Let us dive into it from the perspective of a relativist and motivate it instead of defining it as is

Consider something called a celestial sphere, which contains all the events you can see at one instant of time (in principle - can't do anything if you have myopia, yikes)

This collection of events (from whom the light ray originated) forms a sphere in the past - this can be understood by the equation for a null ray:

dt^2 = constant = dx^2 + dy^2 + dz^2 [This is our good old equation for a sphere]

In many texts you will find the use of stereographic projections - but why do we need it when we just have a sphere already?
